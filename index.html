What were the environmental impacts after the Jas Gripen 39 Crash in Ronneby?

About Saab

Saab is a swedish company that focuses on safety among its citizens. Saabs planes have made a great contribution to the security of many countries by enhancing their military. According to Peter Engberg (Head of Traffic Manigment), the planes have never been shot down in battle. The company made 35.5 Million SEK in sales last year. The money is taxed and is contributed to the welfare of Sweden. Their vision is to constantly adapt and meet the customer needs and requests. They also drive their own policy to ensure that their planes are not used in wrong way by their customers, to quote the “Sustainability fact book 2017”, “Our supplier code of conduct, which is based on the UN Global Compact’s principles on human rights, labour, the environment and anti-corruption, is included in all new agreements and in those that are renegotiated”

But what happens when things go wrong...?

On the 21st of August 2018, a Jas gripen plane had crashed into some birds during a test flight. The plane crashed onto the grounds of Ronneby, in county Blekinge. Luckily, the plane crashed down into a ragged area. This meant that it would be safer rather than if the plane had crashed into a forest, lake or field.The crash was not Saabs fault either, since unexpectedly birds flew into the plane. There wasn’t an internal problem. The defense inspector for environment and health recommended that the elements should be dug down into the ground, this would be the best way to dispose of them. But what are the main materials that needed to be taken care of? And what are their environmental consequences? We can group the materials into two main groups: ”Metallic alloys” and “Toxic substances and Elements”.

Metallic alloys

Aluminum 5052

Description

The Aluminum 5052 alloy is primarily used in aircrafts and marine veichles. In Saab Jas gripen it is used primarily in the front-, center- and back- main plates in the body of the vessel. It is also extremely corrosion resistent.

Alloying

Aluminum 5052 is primarily made up of two elements: Magnesium (~2.5%) and Chromium (~0.23%).
To alloy an element, you first heat up the elements that you want to alloy, the atoms will gain kinetic energy (causing it to move around). When the solutiong is cooled down, the atoms settle in place (because they lose kinetic energy) and evenly form new bonds with the other elemnent. They crystalize and retain all their properties and is considered homogeus, meaning that the atoms are perfectly distributed.

Recycling

The Aluminum is driven to a local treatment plant. The cans are first cleaned, and then melted into iron ingots. As the aluminum is melted, all ink and coating is removed. The iron ingots can later be used to be alloyed, or reused into other aluminum parts.

Ti-6Al-4V

Description

Ti-6Al-4V is a Titanium alloy used in spacecraft, misiles, arcraft (50% of all alloys used are titanium based), armor plating. It is especially good in these areas because of high corrosion resistence, heat resistence and a high strength to density ratio. Jas Gripen 39 uses it in the air canal, bottom armor plating and splice plates.

Alloying

Ti-6Al-4V is primarily made up of two elements: Aluminum (~6.75%) and Vanadium (~4.5%).
To alloy an element, you first heat up the elements that you want to alloy, the atoms will gain kinetic energy (causing it to move around). When the solutiong is cooled down, the atoms settle in place (because they lose kinetic energy) and evenly form new bonds with the other elemnent. They crystalize and retain all their properties and is considered homogeus, meaning that the atoms are perfectly distributed.

Recycling

Titanium can be recycled in two different ways, the most common way is to downgrade Titanium into ferrotitanium. You have to downgrade, since titanium is often treated with oxyge, nitrogen and carbon which makes it hard to recycle. But if it's not, you can recycle it the optimal way. The pieces are checked for alloy purity and then sorted acording to scrap size. The material also has to pass several criteria. Then it is recycled.

Steel

Description

Steel can be very versitile, depending on how you decide to alloy it. Some of the characteristics it can get are: Strength, hardness, resistence to schock and heat, better structure, corrosion resistence and tensile strength. Jas Gripen 39 uses steel in its front shields and as fasteners.

Alloying

Alloy steel consists of carbon steel and optional other elements, manganese, silicon, nickel, titanium, copper, chromium and aluminum.
To alloy an element, you first heat up the elements that you want to alloy, the atoms will gain kinetic energy (causing it to move around). When the solutiong is cooled down, the atoms settle in place (because they lose kinetic energy) and evenly form new bonds with the other elemnent. They crystalize and retain all their properties and is considered homogeus, meaning that the atoms are perfectly distributed.

Recycling

The recyling of steel is divised into different steps, which follow: First you collect steel, this is mainly done from scrap yards. You have to sort out the high-quality steel from the low-quality to ensure that the new product will be of good quality. The steel is crushed into managable batches and then shredded. The scraps are heated up into liquid steel, sorted by their alloys because every alloy has a different melting point. The refining process consists of getting rid of debris and impuriteis is the alloy. There are several methods, like: electrolysis, distillation, zone refining, and chromatography. The molten steel is then cooled down and carefully controlled so that the alloys will retain their properties after they have crystalized.

Toxic substances and Elements

Heavy Metals

Heavy metals Have toxic effects on nearby plants, wildlife and humans. When plants accumulate high levels of heavy metals, we might eat them which would be dangerous for us.

Petroleum Hydrocarbons

Petroleum hydrocarbons may affect plant growth and fertility in the soil. If it makes contact with water, it can affect the ecosystem for the plants and animals that live there.

Brominated Flame Retardants

brominated flame retardants can cause cancer, and may alter liver enzymes, affect thyroid levels.
